Japanese Beef Gyudon

Description

Gyudon is a popular Japanese dish consisting of thinly sliced beef simmered in a savory sauce made from soy sauce, mirin, and sugar, typically served over a bowl of steamed rice. It’s often garnished with onions, pickled ginger, and a raw or cooked egg, offering a comforting, flavorful meal that’s quick to prepare.

To make gyudon, simmer thinly sliced beef and onions in a sauce made from soy sauce, mirin, sugar, and dashi until the beef is tender and flavorful. Serve the beef mixture over a bowl of steamed rice, and garnish with pickled ginger or a raw egg if desired.

Ingredients

Gyudon is one of the most popular rice bowl dishes in Japan. Shall we cook Gyudon tonight?

  • 4 cups cooked rice
  • 6 tablespoons mirin (Japanese sweet wine)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sake
  • 1 onion, cut into wedges
  • 10 ounces beef, thinly sliced
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon pickled ginger (beni shoga), or to taste (Optional)

Steps

Here's a very brief overview of what you can expect when you make homemade japanese beef gyudon:

  1. Divide rice evenly between 4 donburi bowls.
  2. Bring mirin, soy sauce, and sake to a boil in a pot over medium heat. Add onion; cook and stir until onion is translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir in beef; simmer until no longer pink and cooked through, about 5 minutes. Spoon beef and onion over rice using a slotted spoon.
  3. Place eggs in the pot; bring to a boil. Cook until soft boiled, 5 to 7 minutes. Peel; add to bowls. Garnish with pickled ginger.
